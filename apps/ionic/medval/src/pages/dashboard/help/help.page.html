Getting Started

This application is designed to help Orthodontic Clinics and Dental Clinics improve the {{consumerLabel}} experience, by keeping an eye on overall {{consumerLabel}} sentiment, addressing problems with the experience, and focusing on the top reasons for {{consumerLabel}} dissatisfaction. Perception is everything, and so this app includes features to avoid bad online reviews and help happy customers give you positive online reviews with richer content.

This document may use the term 'Orthodontic Clinic', but the features apply equally to General Dentistry as well.

This App should be installed on an iPad and used inside your clinic (e.g. at your reception desk).

If you are wondering why we built this app, scroll down to the 'Why does this app exist' section.

Application Modes

This application has two modes: the Administrative Mode, and Feedback Mode. When you log in, you automatically enter the Administrative Mode, and you see the Dashboard screen. All administrative functions (Account Settings, Staff Information, Reports, Follow-ups, etc.) are accessible from the Dashboard.

You can enter the Feedback Mode by clicking on the Enter Feedback mode button on the Dashboard. This is a one-way 'trap-door'; once you enter Feedback mode, you have to log back in to go to Administrative mode.

Once you enter the Feedback Mode, it is safe to keep this app anywhere in your clinic, at a location that is most convenient for you and your {{consumerLabel}}s.

Feedback Mode

When you enter feedback mode from the dashboard there are two survey types available:

  1. Default, Organic survey: This is the short form {{consumerLabel}}-sentiment survey that can be completed in less than a minute.
  2. 5-minute CAHPS-based survey: This survey includes questions from categories outlined by CAHPS.

The Survey Start page

This page either displays a 'Begin' button or two buttons side-by-side: Default and CAHPS-based. Both the Begin and Default buttons kick off the Organic survey. When a {{consumerLabel}} completes a survey, the app automatically cycles back to the start page, showing the 'Begin' button only. When you use the Magic 'Tool' icon (below), the start page shows side-by-side icons for both survey types.

The Magic 'Tool' Icon in the header

The application header has a 'tool' icon on the top right. It is specifically designed to avoid grabbing a {{consumerLabel}}'s attention when the {{consumerLabel}} is going through the survey. If clicked while in the middle of a survey, the app goes back to the start page, showing side-by-side buttons for both survey types.

Clicking the 'Tool' icon while on the survey start page will log you out. This is useful when you want to go back to Administrator mode.

Survey Questions

All surveys ask the overall favorability question: Would you recommend XYZ Clinic to friends and family?

The Organic survey employs unique survey question types to minimize the amount of time spent by the {{consumerLabel}}, but still useful signals about what is important to them. The question types are mostly graphical, and although text feedback options are provided, they are rarely used by {{consumerLabel}}s.

The CAHPS based survey asks questions about which staff members the user interacted with, ratings for those staff, and questions about the quality of doctors, assistants, and front office.

Survey Questions for DDS role/staff cover the following areas:

  • Overall favorability (e.g.: Would you recommend Dr. XYZ...)
  • Friendliness/Courtsey 
  • Medical Skill
  • Responsiveness - addressing questions/concerns
  • Interest in/focus on positive outcomes

Survey Questions for Orthodontic/Dental Assistants cover the following areas:

  • Overall favorability
  • Friendliness/Courtsey
  • Medical Skill
  • Responsiveness - addressing questions/concerns
  • Interest in/focus on positive outcomes

Survey Questions for Front Office covers:

  • Overall favorability
  • Quality of financial information/payment follow ups
  • Ease of Appointments
  • Wait time
  • Cleanliness

Security

{{consumerLabelUpperCase}} data is never stored on the device; it is immediately encrypted and sent to the backend, and stored in encrypted AWS servers. Data is never sent in plaintext. If someone compromises your device, they cannot access any {{consumerLabel}} data without having your username/password.

{{consumerLabelUpperCase}} Attractions/Features

We've built-in several features to encourage {{consumerLabel}} reviews through Organic surveys. Some of these are enabled by default:

  1. Wheel of Fortune: {{consumerLabelUpperCase}}s who provide a review and are not unhappy with the experience can play a game to win a gift card. Please explore the 'Account Settings' page and tap on the help icons for more help on how to set this up. This feature is disabled by default.
  2. Humor: Laughter is the best medicine. This app includes a screensaver on the 'Start' page while in Feedback Mode and the Thank you page after a survey is completed. While this app is sitting at your reception desk, it will cycle through this humor, putting a smile on your {{consumerLabel}}'s faces! This feature is enabled by default.
  3. Chimes: While on the 'Start' page in Feedback mode, the application will play various short 2 second notes to attract attention and register its presence. This can be an attraction both for office staff and {{consumerLabel}}s who are waiting around the reception area. This feature is enabled by default.
  4. Verbal Thank you: At the Thank you page, this application will use a child-like voice to thank the {{consumerLabel}} for their feedback. This is disabled by default and still in experimental phase. Your feedback is most welcome!

What you can do to encourage more organic feedback

We recommend you encourage your {{consumerLabel}}s to use this app once or twice and after that, rely more on organic traffic (i.e. {{consumerLabel}}s using the app without prompting). Here are some ideas on how to attract {{consumerLabel}} attention organically:

  1. Create a poster (idea #1, idea #2) that you can place above the iPad stand can be a great start.Contact us at questions.revvolve.io if you need help customizing these ideas.
  2. Place a larger poster near your operating area/chairs either on the wall or using a floor stand. This is an effective way to remind {{consumerLabel}}s about this app!
  3. Once a month or quarter, send an email to your {{consumerLabel}}s outlining the improvements you've made, in words your {{consumerLabel}}s can understand. If {{consumerLabel}}s feel that feedback is important to you and you act on it, they will give more feedback!

Staff Settings

You can setup the staff members, assigning the names, titles, and roles. You can transfer pictures to the iPad photo library (or take pictures with the iPad itself if you prefer), and then use those pictures when setting up staff members.

If you setup your staff members, the CAHPS-based survey will gather information about individual staff members the {{consumerLabel}} has worked with.

Reporting

We generate reports on a weekly basis, and sometimes on a daily basis. We use the terms promoters and detractors in this context. Please see this article about the NPS scale and promoter/detractor scores.

There are three kinds of reports:

  1. Summary Reports cover how many survey sessions you had, how many promoters and detractors you have, and the trend in your overall NPS score etc.
  2. Influencer Reports cover how your promoters and detractors think the various metrics for your business affect their ratings. For instance, most promoters may think your top strength is 'Ease of Appointments.' Or your detractors may think that lack of 'genuine interest' in {{consumerLabel}} outcomes is the main reason for their dissatisfaction. These reports are credible if there are at least 50 reviews in the system. These influencer reports can tell you top influencers for the top-level NPS metric, and for overall ratings at the role and staff levels.
  3. Drill down into detailed metrics: You can see how you are doing on individual metrics across roles and staff members.

At the moment, you can request raw data to be emailed to your registered email address, from the App. This feature is likely to require a subscription in the future.

{{consumerLabelUpperCase}} Follow-ups

In the context of this app, there are broadly speaking three cases where {{consumerLabel}} follow-ups become pertinent:

  • A {{consumerLabel}} is very happy, and wants to provide an online review, and provides an email/phone number to receive instructions for a review. In this case, the app automatically follows up with the {{consumerLabel}}.
  • A {{consumerLabel}} is unhappy and requested followup from the contact person. in this case, you need to follow up with a specific individual. You may look up their {{consumerLabel}} records based on their contact information provided.
  • A {{consumerLabel}} is unhappy but did not provide their contact information for follow-up. In this case, you will need to follow up with all {{consumerLabel}}s in a given one-hour period.

For confidentiality reasons, we can't share the exact time of feedback for any {{consumerLabel}}s at this time. Please contact us at questions.revvolve.io for urgent cases where you need more data about {{consumerLabel}} feedback.

Including this App into your day-to-day operations

Placement

There are plenty of options for routine placement, but we highly recommend you keep the iPad stationed at the reception desk or a kiosk stand.

Encouraging {{consumerLabelUpperCase}}s to provide regular feedback

Studies show that {{consumerLabel}}s are highly likely to do a survey if asked by staff in a healthcare environment. However, we encourage you to only do so for the longer CAHPS-based survey. For regular feedback, we recommend that you create a setting where {{consumerLabel}}s provide you with feedback through the organic survey.

  2. Kantek Tablet Stand: This works well if you plan to remove the iPad rarely. It's fairly hard to take out the iPad from this stand.

Why does this app exist?

Traditional survey methodologies don't work very well

On the average, traditional survey methodologies (the web, email, snail-mail) yield limited response rates (less than 20%), are expensive to administer ($30/response) and do not yield timely feedback (36 day response time). Traditional methodologies rely on surveys (based on CAHPS) that take between 5 and 10 minutes to complete. Although longer surveys are important for reporting purposes or certification, they can only be administered once in a while, and are therefore not a reliable way to keep an eye on {{consumerLabel}} sentiment.

How does this app improve over traditional surveys?

This app supports both traditional surveys based on CAHPS and short organic surveys designed to measure {{consumerLabel}} sentiment in the shortest time possible. The default setting for surveys is the short, organic survey.

In-visit Surveys are the future

Conversations with {{consumerLabel}}s during their visit is one of the most effective methods to gain feedback, make improvements and close the loop. This app is a great way to start and sustain that conversation over the long term.

We have great Online Reviews! Do we need this app?

If you are receiving great Online Reviews on a regular basis, that's awesome, and you may not need this app. However, most Doctors agree that even one or two bad online reviews can have a detrimental impact on their work, and the only remedy is to have as many great reviews as possible. 60% of {{consumerLabel}}s pay more attention to actual {{consumerLabel}} comments than the star ratings. This app provides the following features to address these issues:

  1. The app is proactive about getting more reviews from your most satisfied {{consumerLabel}}s.
  2. This app helps {{consumerLabel}}s provide richer content in their reviews.
  3. Providing an online review for Google can require searching for the page, finding the right site, etc. This app makes all that easier for {{consumerLabel}}s.
  4. You can configure the app to focus on the channels (facebook/google/yelp) that you care about.

But we only get a few complaints a month

The impact of dissatisfied {{consumerLabel}}s is very high, and not everyone complains or lets you know. According to most experts in the field, for everyone who complains, 20 dissatisfied {{consumerLabel}}s do not1. Of those dissatisfied {{consumerLabel}}s who do not complain, 90% will not refer their friends or family to your clinic.

The cost to your business from unhappy {{consumerLabel}}s is high. It costs ten times as much to attract new {{consumerLabel}}s as it does to keep current ones, and changing a poor image takes 5-10 years on average. It's easy to slide into complacency, even for the best-run businesses!
